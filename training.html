Training Process

Your Path to Becoming an LAPD Patrol Officer

Every LAPD officer undergoes a comprehensive and rigorous training process to ensure they're prepared for the challenges of law enforcement. This journey transforms recruits into competent, confident patrol officers ready to serve Los Angeles. Below is the training pathway you'll follow on your way to becoming a solo patrol officer.

Your Training Journey

1

Academy Exam

The first crucial step in your LAPD career begins with passing the academy examination. This comprehensive exam tests your knowledge, physical fitness, and readiness for law enforcement training. Success here demonstrates your commitment and preparation for the rigorous training ahead.

2

RA I - First Supervised Ride Along

Your first ride along with a Training Officer is your introduction to real patrol work. You'll be expected to wear the correct uniform, carry proper gear, and demonstrate readiness. This ride along allows the Training Bureau to assess your foundational patrol skills and identify areas for improvement.

3

RA II - Second Supervised Ride Along

During your second ride along, expectations rise. You'll be expected to demonstrate increased confidence, better preparation, and the ability to handle unexpected situations. This is where you prove you've learned from RA I and are progressing toward solo patrol readiness.

4

Final Evaluation

This is your moment to shine. During the final evaluation, your Training Officer will step back and let you take charge. They expect you to demonstrate solid knowledge of procedures, show good judgment, and handle situations independently. The Training Officer will only intervene if absolutely necessary.

5

Pass Evaluation

Congratulations! After passing your final evaluation, you're cleared to patrol as a Solo Patrol Officer. You'll still be required to wear the Class A uniform while you're under reduced supervision. This is the beginning of your career as an independent patrol officer.

6

Promote to Patrol Officer II

After passing your evaluation and serving your time as a Solo Patrol Officer, you'll be eligible for promotion. When the next promotion cycle arrives, you can advance to Patrol Officer II, opening the door to further specialization and career advancement within the LAPD.

💡 Key Success Tips

Pay close attention during your academy training. Show up prepared for each ride along with the proper uniform and equipment. Be ready to learn from your Training Officer's feedback. Demonstrate confidence, sound judgment, and a commitment to community safety. Most importantly, remain dedicated to upholding the values of integrity, professionalism, and service that define the LAPD.
