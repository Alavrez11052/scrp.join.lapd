LAPD Structure

Specialized Divisions

Find Your Unit

The LAPD is made up of many specialized divisions, each playing a distinct and vital role in keeping Los Angeles safe. After completing your training and gaining patrol experience, you may be eligible to apply for placement in one of the units below.

🔍

Detective Services Bureau

Handle major crime investigations across Los Angeles. Detectives gather evidence, conduct interviews, and build cases that bring criminals to justice.

🚁

Air Support Division

Provide aerial support and surveillance from above the city. Air Support assists ground units with tracking suspects, monitoring scenes, and rapid area coverage.

📡

Communications Division

Operate the city's emergency dispatch services. Dispatchers are the critical link between the public and officers in the field, managing calls and coordinating response 24/7.

🛡️

Gang Enforcement Detail

Conduct targeted gang suppression operations across Los Angeles. This unit works to disrupt criminal street gang activity and reduce violent crime in affected communities.

🧪

Gang & Narcotics Division

Investigate narcotics trafficking and organized crime networks. GND works closely with federal partners to dismantle drug organizations operating in and around Los Angeles.

⚔️

Metro Division

Provide specialized tactical support to patrol operations across the city. Metro includes SWAT capabilities and responds to the most high-risk and complex situations.

🚦

Traffic Division

Enforce roadway safety and traffic laws across Los Angeles. Traffic officers investigate collisions, manage DUI enforcement, and keep the city's roads moving safely.

How to Join a Division

Specialized divisions are not entry-level positions. To be considered, you must first complete the full training process — including the academy, both ride-alongs, and your final evaluation.

Once you've earned your Solo Patrol clearance and been promoted to Patrol Officer II, you may express interest in a specialized unit. Division placement is based on performance, department need, and your Training Officer's recommendation.

Eligibility Requirements

  • Completed academy examination
  • Passed both supervised ride-alongs
  • Passed final evaluation
  • Cleared for solo patrol
  • Promoted to Patrol Officer II or above
  • Positive recommendation from Training Officer
