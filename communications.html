Training Process

Your Path to the Badge
1

Academy Exam

Pass the academy examination. This is your first and most fundamental milestone — demonstrating the baseline knowledge and commitment required of every LAPD officer.

2

Ride-Along I

Your first supervised ride-along. During this ride-along you will be expected to have on the correct uniform, have the correct gear, and be prepared. This ride-along is generally a way for the Training Bureau to see where you stand as a Patrol Officer and what you need to improve on.

3

Ride-Along II

Your second supervised ride-along. During your second ride-along you will be expected to be more confident, prepared, and ready for anything thrown your way. Growth from RA I is expected and evaluated.

4

Final Evaluation

During your evaluation your Training Officer will put you in charge of everything. They will expect you to have a good knowledge of what's happening, how to do things, and they should only ever step in when absolutely necessary.

5

Solo Patrol Clearance

After passing your Final Evaluation you will be allowed to patrol as a Solo Patrol Officer. You will still be required to wear the Class A uniform, though you'll be under less supervision.

6

Promote to Patrol Officer II

After passing your evaluation you must wait for the next promotion cycle to be promoted to Patrol Officer II — the first major rank advancement in your LAPD career.

Training Tips

  • Always arrive in correct uniform and gear — first impressions matter
  • Study department procedures and call codes before each ride-along
  • Communicate clearly and professionally at all times
  • Ask questions — training officers expect initiative
  • Be prepared for anything during your final evaluation
